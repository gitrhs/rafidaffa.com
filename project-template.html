Project Overview

Whispers of the Path is a virtual reality game developed as a project for WIG3004 - Virtual Reality at University Malaya. Working within a one-week timeframe, we created a complete VR adventure experience from concept to playable demo.
A lost soul wakes up in the Realm of Echoes, a world between sleep and consciousness. A lifeless tree and a wise peasant guide the player to find an ancient portal that leads to the Singing Stone. The Singing Stone is the way for players to return home.
The primary objective of this game is to explore the open world of the Realm of Echoes, interact with mystical elements, and find the ancient portal leading to the Singing Stone to escape this dreamlike dimension. Players move through a VR environment where they discover the story through exploration. The game focuses on environmental storytelling where players learn about the world by walking around and finding clues.

Characters & Avatars

We created virtual versions of ourselves as the main characters. Alyssa's avatar wears traditional batik clothing, and Rafi's avatar wears Indonesian cultural clothing. We also made The Peasant, an NPC character who guides players and explains the secrets of the Realm of Echoes.
We built the characters by first creating an armature with bones for movement. We added bones for the head, torso, arms, and legs so the characters can move naturally. We used weight painting to control which parts of the character move with each bone. We tested the character movement in pose mode to make sure everything worked correctly before putting them in the game.

Game World & Assets

The VR world looks like real environments but with some fantasy elements. We added grass and trees that move in the wind to make the world feel more alive. The environment mixes normal nature with fantasy elements from the Realm of Echoes.
The game world includes grass and evergreen trees that move with wind animation. We added fantasy elements like the Rock Portal and Singing Stones that are important for gameplay. We also built rock formations with lighting and shadows, plus different textures to make the environment look varied.
Players can walk around the entire game world freely while following the main story to reach the Singing Stone. This open-world setup lets players explore at their own speed without being forced to follow a specific path.

Immersive Experience

The game uses dynamic lighting and shadows to create depth. The graphics run at up to 120fps so players don't get motion sickness. We added environmental effects and atmospheric rendering to make the world look more realistic.
We added different footstep sounds that change depending on what surface players walk on - grass, water, or other materials. Background music plays throughout the game, and we included character dialogue sounds and special audio effects for the portals. These sounds help make the world feel more real.
The VR controllers vibrate when players enter the portal, giving physical feedback. For character conversations, we used Animal Crossing-style sound effects instead of full voice acting. Players move around using teleportation, which prevents motion sickness. The story is told through the environment - players learn by exploring and finding things in the world.
This VR game runs on VR headsets with performance up to 120fps for comfortable gameplay. We used teleportation for player movement to avoid motion sickness. The audio system uses 3D spatial sound so players can hear where sounds come from in the virtual space. The graphics use dynamic lighting that changes based on the game environment.

Game Ending

The game ends when players enter the final portal. This triggers an ending video that shows the player waking up from a dream. The twist reveals that the entire game was actually a dream. This ending explains why the Realm of Echoes felt strange and connects the dream-like game world back to reality.

Gameplay Demo

This project challenged us to create a complete VR experience in just one week. We learned how to make a VR game that runs smoothly while telling a story under tight time constraints. The short development period taught us to prioritize essential features and work efficiently as a team.
We learned to create VR movement that doesn't make players sick, build character animations that work in VR, and design a game world that feels both realistic and fantastical. The project showed us how to use environmental storytelling in VR and the technical challenges of VR development.
