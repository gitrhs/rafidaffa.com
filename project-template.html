Project Overview

UM Notes is a digital platform built exclusively for students of the University of Malaya (UM). It serves as a resource hub where students can share lecture notes, past papers, study guides, and even general files that may be useful to the academic community. Access is restricted to UM students, who log in using their university email accounts. The project was developed as part of the PPI-UM work program and the System Analysis and Design course, where I worked as both full-stack developer and UI/UX designer.

How it works?

The platform allows students to upload files through a simple process. A file is first hosted on a cloud storage service such as Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox, and then linked through the UM Notes dashboard. Once a student submits the file, it is reviewed by a contributor or admin before being published to ensure quality and relevance. This verification step keeps the platform organized and reliable for all users.

Community Features

UM Notes emphasizes collaboration and contribution. Students who upload files earn reward points, which can be collected and exchanged for items through the rewards page. Contributors play a special role in the ecosystem by verifying pending files, creating new folders, and ensuring the platform remains structured and easy to navigate. Their names are displayed on the resources they approve, giving them visibility and recognition within the UM community. A leaderboard highlights the top contributors, encouraging active participation and fostering a sense of ownership.

Impact

Since its launch, UM Notes has become one of the reliable platforms for UM students to access course references. It centralizes resources that would otherwise be scattered across private groups and personal drives, while also motivating students to give back to the community. By combining accessibility, collaboration, and gamified rewards, the platform creates a sustainable model for knowledge-sharing within the university.
